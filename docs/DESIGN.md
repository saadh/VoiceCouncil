## Problem Statement

People bomb high-stakes interviews because they can't practice under real pressure. Mock interviews with humans cost $150+/hr and aren't available at 11pm the night before. Existing AI prep tools are mostly text chatbots — they don't recreate the feeling of a live panel firing follow-ups at you.

VoiceCouncil is a voice-first AI interview panel: a candidate uploads a job description + resume, gets grilled by a 3-persona panel over a live voice call, and walks away with a scored feedback report. Lead vertical: tech/behavioral job interviews. Expansion: consulting case interviews → PhD viva → medical boards (same engine, swappable persona pack + question bank).

Built for a one-day Voice AI Hackathon (~5 hours of build time). Must demo a live voice call that ends in a visible scorecard.

## What Makes This Cool

- **The "it read my resume" moment.** Paste a real JD + resume, and the panel asks questions tailored to *that* candidate — including the gap on the CV. That's the demo beat that makes judges lean in.
- **You watch the council confer — twice.** Before the call, the three models hold a "war room": each prepares its focus area and questions, and a synthesized Interview Plan appears on screen. After the call, they each grade you and a synthesized scorecard appears. Two visible multi-model moments (Plan + Scorecard), both offline so they never lag the voice.
- **The metaphor is the architecture.** A real panel literally is multiple people asking from different angles. The multi-model council isn't a gimmick bolted on — it's a 1:1 model of the real thing.
- **You leave with an artifact.** The scorecard (communication, depth, structure, red flags + top-3 fixes) is the thing users keep and come back for.

## Premises

1. **The council should be genuinely multi-model — but only where it's visible.** Judges can't *hear* whether 3 panelists are 3 models or 1 model in 3 hats. Real multi-model buys exactly one thing: a credible Nebius-track story. So spend multi-model on the scorecard (seen on screen), not on every spoken turn (where it adds latency and failure surface). — AGREED, resolved via hybrid architecture.
2. **The real product is the resume/JD-tailored questions + the end scorecard, not the panel headcount.** — AGREED.
3. **Job-interview vertical for the live demo; medical as expansion only.** — AGREED.
4. **The win condition is a live voice call that ends in a visible scorecard.** Everything else is decoration. — AGREED.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Persona council, one model (minimal viable)
Single Nebius model, 3 system-prompted personas take turns; scorecard from the same model.
- Effort: S · Risk: Low
- Pros: fewest moving parts, lowest latency, demo-safe; still sounds like a panel.
- Cons: weak Nebius-track story (one model); the "council" is cosmetic.
- Reuses: single Vapi assistant + one model endpoint.

### Approach B: Real multi-model council (ideal / Nebius-aligned)
A different Nebius model per panelist; every spoken turn hits that panelist's own model; end deliberation fans out to all.
- Effort: L · Risk: Med-High
- Pros: strongest possible Nebius narrative; genuinely diverse voices.
- Cons: per-turn latency hurts the voice feel; most moving parts to break live.
- Reuses: Nebius multi-model API.

### Approach C: Hybrid — one model live, council for the verdict (chosen)
One fast Nebius model drives the live interview turns (low latency, demo-safe). At session end, fan out to 3 different Nebius models that each grade the candidate independently, then synthesize one scorecard shown on screen as "the panel confers."
- Effort: M · Risk: Low-Med
- Pros: live latency stays tight; real multi-model exactly where judges can see it; strong Nebius story; the deliberation becomes the visual moment.
- Cons: two code paths (live vs verdict); must design the fan-out/synthesis prompt.
- Reuses: single live Vapi assistant; Nebius fan-out only at the end.

## Recommended Approach

**Approach C (Hybrid).** It's the only option that keeps the live call demo-safe *and* delivers a real multi-model story where the Nebius judges can see it. The end-of-session fan-out turns the "council deliberates" mechanic into a visible payoff (the scorecard) instead of dead air mid-conversation. Confirmed by the user in office hours.

### Architecture — three phases

The product runs in three phases. Multi-model work lives in the two OFFLINE phases
(Prep, Verdict) where latency doesn't hurt the voice; the LIVE phase uses one fast
model. Prep and Verdict are the SAME operation — "fan out to N Nebius models, then
synthesize" — so build that helper ONCE and call it twice.

**Phase 1 — PREP (offline, multi-model). The council "war room."**
The three models hold a one-round negotiation so they genuinely agree on lanes —
not a referee imposing it. Personas are fixed (HM / Expert / Bar-raiser); what they
negotiate is FOCUS-AREA coverage so the three don't all ask the same thing.
```
Intake (role required; paste JD optional; paste resume optional)
  → ROUND 1 (parallel): fan out to the 3 Nebius models, EACH in its assigned
    persona. Each proposes its own focus area + a seeded question bank.
  → ROUND 2 (parallel): each model is shown the OTHERS' Round-1 proposals and
    revises — claims its lane, drops overlaps, fills gaps ("you take system
    design, I'll take the conflict story").
  → light synthesis locks the final division and assembles the plan.
  → output: one Interview Plan (per-persona focus + question bank). Store in
    Insforge AND surface the Round-1→Round-2 exchange on the Plan screen — the
    visible negotiation is a multi-agent demo asset.
  → FALLBACK (if time is tight): drop Round 2 and let synthesis divide coverage
    (independent + synthesis). Still non-overlapping; just less visibly "agreed."
```

**Phase 2 — LIVE INTERVIEW (one fast model, low latency).**
```
Vapi (voice call, turn-taking, barge-in)
  → custom LLM endpoint on Insforge
      • CONTRACT: Vapi requires an OpenAI-compatible POST /chat/completions that
        STREAMS SSE. The Insforge function must stream tokens back (verify this
        works early — see Risks). If Insforge can't stream cleanly, fall back to
        Approach A: point Vapi's assistant directly at a Nebius OpenAI-compatible
        base URL and use Insforge for storage only.
      • EXECUTES THE PLAN: 1 fast Nebius model, system-prompted with the Interview
        Plan from Phase 1, runs the 3 personas reading from the question bank and
        improvising follow-ups. It emits a structured tag (e.g. [HM]/[EXPERT]/[BAR])
        at the start of each turn so the dashboard can highlight the active speaker.
```

**Phase 3 — VERDICT (offline, multi-model). The council confers.**
```
Vapi's `end-of-call-report` webhook POSTs the full transcript to Insforge (that
transcript is the source of truth — do NOT rebuild it).
  → reuse the Phase 1 fan-out helper: 3 different Nebius models grade IN PARALLEL,
    each independently → 1 synthesis pass → scorecard. If any grader fails/times
    out, synthesize from whichever returned — never block on one model.
  → stored in Insforge; dashboard reads it via Insforge realtime subscription, or
    polls the `scorecards` row if realtime slips.

Insforge stores: resume/JD, interview plan, session transcript, scorecard.
```

### Panel personas (the on-screen visual)
- **Hiring Manager** — behavioral ("tell me about a time…")
- **Domain Expert** — deep technical follow-ups
- **Bar-raiser / Skeptic** — pressure-tests weak answers

Scorecard dimensions: communication, depth, structure, red flags + top-3 concrete fixes.

## Open Questions

- **Intake — RESOLVED:** structured form before the call — role (required), paste JD (optional), paste resume (optional). Take whatever's provided; JD is richest, role string is the floor. Optional spoken opener ("What role are you interviewing for, and where?") is a nice-to-have layered on top, not a replacement. File upload + parse deferred.
- **Which 3 Nebius models for the scorecard fan-out?** Pick 3 visibly distinct ones (e.g. a Llama, a Qwen, a DeepSeek) so the "different graders" story is real. Confirm availability against Nebius Token Factory's catalog at the event.
- **Web call vs phone call for the demo:** Vapi supports both. Web embed is more reliable on conference wifi; a phone call is a stronger theatrical beat. Decide based on venue network.
- **Voice differentiation per persona:** nice-to-have (distinct Vapi voices), not required for v1.

## Success Criteria

- A judge completes a live voice mock interview (≥3 tailored questions with at least one follow-up) end to end without a crash.
- An Interview Plan (per-persona focus + questions) is generated from the role/JD/resume by ≥3 distinct Nebius models and shown on screen before the call.
- The panel's live questions visibly follow that plan and reference the resume/JD.
- A scorecard appears on screen within ~30s of ending (3 graders run in parallel; fill the wait with the "panel is conferring" state), produced by ≥3 distinct Nebius models — degrading gracefully to fewer if one fails.
- The whole loop is reproducible on demand (new session ID per run; no shared state collides between back-to-back demos).
- Project is registered against all three sponsor tracks (Vapi, Nebius, Insforge).

## Distribution Plan

Web service. Vapi hosts the voice agent; the custom LLM endpoint + dashboard deploy on Insforge. No app-store/binary distribution needed. For judging: a single shareable URL (and/or a Vapi phone number). Existing Insforge deployment pipeline covers hosting.

## Risks (from adversarial review)

- **#1 Live-path streaming (highest risk).** Vapi↔endpoint and endpoint↔Nebius both need to stream for the voice to feel live. If the Insforge function can't stream SSE cleanly, the call lags. **Mitigation:** spike in Step 0; Approach A (Vapi → Nebius directly) is the budgeted escape hatch.
- **Nebius model availability on stage.** A cold/rate-limited grader breaks the headline multi-model beat. **Mitigation:** verify 3 model IDs early; pre-warm with a dummy call right before the demo.
- **Scorecard latency.** 3 graders + synthesis won't hit 10s sequentially. **Mitigation:** run graders in parallel, target ~30s, cover with the "conferring" state.
- **Decoration creep.** The "watch the council confer" animation and per-persona active-speaker highlighting are nice-to-have, not win-condition. **Mitigation:** timebox; a static "Deliberating…" → reveal is acceptable if time slips.
- **Credit burn.** $50 Vapi / $25 Insforge is thin across a day of rehearsals. **Mitigation:** glance at Vapi minute spend during practice.
